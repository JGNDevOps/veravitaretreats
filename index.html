The People Behind Vera Vita

Two women.
Two lifetimes of practice.

Maria and Nadia are not wellness influencers. They are clinically grounded, deeply experienced practitioners who have spent their careers in service to other women.

Maria
Jurado

Cyclical Psychologist · Madrid, Spain
"The work is not about becoming someone different. It is about understanding yourself so completely that you stop needing to escape who you are."

A life devoted to women's inner worlds

Maria grew up in Spain and has spent her entire professional life asking one question: why do women — even the strong, capable, accomplished ones — so often feel fundamentally alone in their pain?

With her own private practice in Madrid and collaborative work with some of the city's most respected psychology centres, Maria has spent decades sitting with women through the moments that define a life: depression that no one sees, the quiet collapse of a marriage, the identity loss of menopause, the grief of low self-esteem, and the complexity of couples who have forgotten how to reach each other.

Her approach is grounded in cyclical psychology — a framework that understands women's emotional and psychological experience as inherently rhythmic, context-dependent, and biological as well as psychological. It is not a trend. It is a lifetime of observation turned into method.

Her philosophy in practice

"I am not here to fix anyone. I am here to create enough safety that a woman can finally look honestly at what she has been carrying — and decide what she actually wants to do with it. The answers are always already there. My work is simply to help them become visible."

In the retreat setting, Maria leads group psychological workshops, facilitated reflection sessions, and is available for private one-on-one consultations depending on tier. Her sessions are warm but precise — she does not let comfort become avoidance.

Nadia
Zervou

Wellness Coach · Athens, Greece
"The body remembers what the mind has learned to ignore. Our work is not to force it open — but to create enough stillness that it can finally speak."

A lifetime in the language of the body

Nadia has always believed that the body remembers what the mind tries to forget. Based in Greece and working across Europe, she has spent decades helping women reconnect with their physical selves — not through punishment or performance, but through genuine care and attentive listening.

As a health and wellness coach specialising in meditation, therapeutic movement, and natural restoration techniques, Nadia brings a philosophy that is rare in its simplicity: the body, when listened to, knows exactly what it needs. Her clients are not instructed to perform wellness — they are guided into it.

Her background draws from multiple disciplines: meditative practices, therapeutic stretching and somatic movement, breathwork traditions, and natural health approaches built over years of hands-on client work. She does not follow trends. She follows results.

In the retreat, Nadia leads morning movement and meditation sessions, breathwork workshops, and somatic integration exercises designed to work in rhythm with Maria's psychological sessions. The body opens first — creating space for the mind to follow.

Why It Works

The thinking behind
the structure

01

Body before mind

Nadia's sessions always precede Maria's. Somatic work relaxes the defensive structures that prevent genuine psychological engagement. By the time Maria begins, the body has already created the conditions for honesty.

02

Small groups by design

A maximum of twelve women. Not as a marketing differentiator — as a therapeutic requirement. Real trust does not form in large groups. The work requires a circle small enough that every voice is known.

03

Integration, not just experience

Most retreats end when you leave. Vera Vita is designed to begin equipping you on day one for what happens after day four. The toolkit, follow-up sessions, and forward plan are core to the programme.
